About the Gardens


Discover Paddington Reservoir Gardens—a hidden gem in Sydney's inner east where heritage meets modern tranquility. Once a 19th-century water reservoir, this stunning sunken garden, restored and reopened in 2009, resembles the ancient baths of Caracalla in Rome.

With its blend of historic architecture, striking arches, ironwork, and lush green spaces, the Gardens are a haven for art lovers, photographers, history enthusiasts, and anyone seeking a peaceful escape. Whether you're here to admire the heritage, explore community events, or simply relax, Paddington Reservoir Gardens offers a serene and enchanting experience unlike any other in the city.

Reservoir History


Paddington Reservoir, listed on the NSW State Heritage Register, was once vital to Sydney’s third water supply system. Built to store water from Lords Dam at Botany Bay, it complemented the nearby Crown Street Reservoir. Construction on Paddington began in 1864 and was completed in 1866, creating a reservoir capable of holding 2 million gallons. Operational by 1878, it supplied the growing city until 1899, when the Centennial Park Reservoir took over.

After decommissioning, the reservoir saw varied uses, including as a garage and workshop from 1914 to 1934, and later as a service station. Over time, neglect led to structural decay, and by the 1990s, significant portions had deteriorated, with roof collapses in 1990 and 1993 closing it to the public. Despite this decline, the site’s rooftop hosted Walter Read Reserve from 1953, with the John Thompson Reserve added in 1976.

In the early 2000s, a visionary plan emerged to transform the forgotten reservoir into a public garden that would honor its heritage while bringing new life to the space. Led by Tonkin Zulaikha Greer Architects, the restoration was completed in 2006 with a focus on both conservation and innovation.

Key features like brick arches, ironwork, and structural beams were meticulously preserved, while parts were left untouched to emphasize its historic essence. Lush gardens now contrast beautifully with the industrial textures, and the design includes tranquil pools, sunken gardens, and open spaces for relaxation.

This remarkable transformation of Paddington Reservoir turned it into a beloved community space, a place for art exhibitions, photography, and events that celebrate Sydney’s heritage. The Gardens now stand as a testament to the power of adaptive reuse, blending past and present to create an oasis that is both historic and deeply rooted in the urban fabric of modern Sydney.
